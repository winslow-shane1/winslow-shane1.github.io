GIS Poster Projects

Does Suburbia show the Racial-Spatial Divide?

This poster was presented at the 2018 American Society of Criminology (ASC) annual conference. This research seeks to explore whether the Racial-Spatial Divide presented by Peterson & Krivo extends outside the urban to the suburban environment. It looks at the county population surrounding Philadelphia, PA and Camden, NJ.

Where Have All the Stairways Gone?: "Re"-examining the Justification of Crime for the Removal of Pedestrian Connections

This poster presentation was delivered at the 2016 American Association of Geographers (AAG) in San Fransico, California. This research explores the justification for removal of public stairway infrastructure and whether it reduced crime based upon theories of Crime Prevention through Environmental Design (CPTED) and target-hardening.

GIS Presentation Projects

Cause For Closure: Exploratory Multilevel Binary Logistic Regression on the Prediction of Stairway Closures in Cincinnati, Ohio

This powerpoint was used to provide visual support in a presentation on research that explored the various environmental factors that promoted to public pedestrian stairway closures (i.e. lighting, trash, neighborhood socio-economic status, means of access to points of interest, and stairway condition in the city of Cincinnati. Based upon the status of these variables it also predicted future closures using binary logisitic regression.

Regress Without Reason: A Study of Urban Planning and Crime Prevention in Cincinnati, Ohio

This powerpoint was used to provide visual support in a presentaion on research that observed whether or not stairway closures were an adequate form of crime prevention based on the theoretical principle of Crime Prevention through Environmental Design (CPTED). This was used in conjuction to the poster presentation found above.

Collins Street Stairway Project

This powerpoint was used to provide visual support in a presentation on a specific pedestrian stairway called the Collins Street stairway. Collins street was a specific topic of interest because despite being closed for purposes of crime prevention and target-hardening for the Keys Crescent private community, there was no significant change in crime before or after closure, yet blocked access points for individuals to important public transportation routes. Due to this, it was a very contentious policy issue for the local Cincinnati neighborhood.

Publications

The Cost of Empire: How the Aztecs Political and Economic System Facilitated Spanish Conquest

This paper was published in the journal Perspective in History. It posits that weak points in the Aztec political and socio-economic structures could be exploited by the Spanish Conquistadors, led by Hernan Cortes. It contributes to original research in Mesoamerican history by exploring what weak points in the Aztec society could be exploited by the Spanish colonizers.

Masters Thesis: Exploring the Relationship between Pedestrian Accessibility and Crime Prevention

This was my Master Thesis to graduate with a Masters degree in Geography with a focus on Geographic Information Systems (GIS) from the University of Cincinnati. This research is the culmination of research presented above with the results on how crime may or may not be reduced based on Crime Prevention through Environmental Design. It utilizes a combination of buffers areas, geolocated crime positions, and a temporal study on crime before and after the closure of pedestrian infrastructure to indicate whether the closure was effective in redcucing crime for those streel-level areas.

El Cortes Verdadero

This article was published in the Forum, a Sigma Iota national foreign language honor-society publication. It was written entirely in Spanish and briefly explores the character of Cortes the man, and his personality, beyond his infamy as a Spanish conquistador.



Stairways to Salvation

This article was published in the Northern Kentucky Heritage Magazine Vol. XXI No. 1. It explores the public stairway infrastructure around Mt. Adams and their importance to the Catholic community, spefically looking at the Immaculata cathedral steps and the various other access points to the Mt. Adams historical and contemporary community.

Step by Step: The Creation of Original Independent Undergraduate Research

This arictle was published in the Middle Grounds journal, based out of the North Hennipen University in Minnesota. It specifically outlines a process for original udergraduate independent research and the trials and lessons from such an endeavor.
